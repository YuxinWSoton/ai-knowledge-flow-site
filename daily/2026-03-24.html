2. Covariant Symplectic Geometry of Classical Particles

  • 链接：https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21934v1
  • 来源：arxiv
  • 摘要：We investigate the tension between symplecticity and gauge covariance in classical Hamiltonian mechanics. The pursuit of manifest covariance over manifest symplecticity results in a unique geometric formulation. Firstly, covariant yet non-canonical coordinates are employed by adopting Souriau's approach to minimal coupling. Secondly, covariant yet non-coordinate frames arise from Ehresmann connections in phase space. Thirdly, the concept of covariant Poisson bracket is introduced, facilitating direct derivations of covariant equations of motion. In this way, we establish manifestly covariant Hamiltonian formulations of particles coupled to background gauge and gravitational fields, with or without spin. The variational principle and path integral origins of our framework are also explicated.
  • 易漏细节梳理：

covariant坐标是非规范的，而covariant框架则不依赖于坐标。covariant泊松括号有助于推导运动方程，因此在形式上优先考虑协变性而非辛可积性。为了实现这一目标，Souriau的方法被用于最小耦合，而Ehresmann联络则提供了相空间中的协变框架。通过明确地将变分原理和路径积分起源加以解释，这种形式不仅能够处理粒子与背景场的耦合，还能包括自旋在协变哈密顿形式中。此外，研究还探讨了辛可积性和规范协变性之间的张力，确保了理论的完整性和一致性。

  • 关键点（易漏细节）：

  • the variational principle and path integral origins are explicated;

3. Abelian Galois cohomology of quasi-connected reductive groups

  • 链接：https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22059v1
  • 来源：arxiv
  • 摘要：In 1999 Labesse introduced quasi-connected reductive groups and investigated their abelian Galois cohomology over local and global fields of characteristic 0. We (1) generalize some of the constructions of Labesse from quasi-connected reductive groups to arbitrary reductive groups, not necessarily connected or quasi-connected; (2) generalize results of Labesse on the abelian Galois cohomology of quasi-connected reductive groups to the case of local and global fields of arbitrary characteristic; and (3) investigate the functoriality properties of the abelian Galois cohomology. In particular, we introduce the notion of a principal homomorphism of quasi-connected reductive groups, and show that if G is a quasi-connected reductive group over a local or global field $k$ of positive characteristic, then the first Galois cohomology set H^1(k,G) has a canonical structure of abelian group, which is functorial with respect to principal homomorphisms.
  • 易漏细节梳理：

首先，研究关注于特征为正的域上的可约李群，并引入了主同态的概念，这是研究中一个新颖的元素。其次，这些主同态对于可约李群的泛函性质至关重要，而泛函性质是研究的核心。此外，研究将泛函性质扩展到了任意特征的局部和全局域，这一扩展使得研究更加广泛和深入。因此，特征为正的可约李群的泛函性质研究不仅限于拟连通群，而是推广到了任意可约李群。因此，这些研究对于表示论和代数几何具有重要意义，特别是通过首次探讨了阿贝尔伽罗西上同调的泛函性质，进一步揭示了主同态在这一领域的关键作用。

  • 关键点（易漏细节）：
  • positive characteristic fields may have a canonical abelian group structure.
  • principal homomorphisms are crucial for the functoriality properties.
  • generalizations are made to arbitrary reductive groups, not just quasi-connected ones.
  • the first Galois cohomology set is functorial with respect to principal homomorphisms.
  • quasi-connected reductive groups were introduced by Labesse in 1999.
  • the study extends to local and global fields of arbitrary characteristic.
  • the work investigates the functoriality properties of abelian Galois cohomology.
  • the notion of a principal homomorphism is newly introduced.
  • the results are significant for representation theory and algebraic geometry.

4. Fast-DiM: Towards Fast Diffusion Morphs

  • 链接：https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.09484v3
  • 来源：arxiv
  • 摘要：Diffusion Morphs (DiM) are a recent state-of-the-art method for creating high quality face morphs; however, they require a high number of network function evaluations (NFE) to create the morphs. We propose a new DiM pipeline, Fast-DiM, which can create morphs of a similar quality but with fewer NFE. We investigate the ODE solvers used to solve the Probability Flow ODE and the impact they have on the the creation of face morphs. Additionally, we employ an alternative method for encoding images into the latent space of the Diffusion model by solving the Probability Flow ODE as time runs forwards. Our experiments show that we can reduce the NFE by upwards of 85% in the encoding process while experiencing only 1.6\% reduction in Mated Morph Presentation Match Rate (MMPMR). Likewise, we showed we could cut NFE, in the sampling process, in half with only a maximal reduction of 0.23% in MMPMR.
